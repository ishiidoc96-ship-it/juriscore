The Constitution of Kenya

2010 — with amendments through 2024

Article 1

All sovereign power belongs to the people

(1) All sovereign power belongs to the people of Kenya and shall be exercised only in accordance with this Constitution.

(2) The people may exercise their sovereign power directly or through their democratically elected representatives.

(3) This Constitution is the Supreme law of the Republic and any other law inconsistent with it is void to the extent of the inconsistency.

Article 2

Supremacy of this Constitution

(1) This Constitution is the Supreme law of the Republic and any other law inconsistent with it is void to the extent of the inconsistency.

(2) Any person, a State organ or county government has the right to institute court proceedings, claiming that this Constitution has been contravened.

Article 3

Defence of this Constitution

(1) Every person has a duty to respect, uphold and defend this Constitution.

(2) Any attempt to establish a government otherwise than in compliance with this Constitution is unlawful.

Republic v Director of Public Prosecutions & Another

[2020] eKLR — High Court at Nairobi

Constitutional Law Criminal Procedure

Facts

The applicant sought orders to enforce fundamental rights and freedoms under the Constitution. The matter involved the interpretation of Article 43 on economic and social rights.

Issue

Whether the state's failure to provide adequate healthcare facilities constitutes a violation of the right to the highest attainable standard of health under Article 43(1)(a).

Holding

The court held that the right to health is justiciable and the state has an immediate obligation to take reasonable measures within its available resources to achieve the full realization of this right.

Ratio Decidendi

The doctrine of progressive realization does not render socioeconomic rights non-justiciable.

JUDGMENT OF THE COURT

INTRODUCTION

1. This judgment is delivered pursuant to the parties' written submissions and oral arguments presented on 15th October 2019.

FACTUAL BACKGROUND

2. The applicant is a citizen of Kenya who brought this matter in the public interest seeking declarations and orders regarding the enforcement of fundamental rights and freedoms guaranteed under the Constitution of Kenya, 2010.

ISSUES FOR DETERMINATION

3. The court identifies the following issues for determination:

(a) Whether the right to health under Article 43(1)(a) is justiciable;

(b) Whether the state has an immediate obligation to provide minimum core health services;

(c) What remedies are appropriate.

ANALYSIS AND DETERMINATION

4. On the first issue, the court has carefully considered the submissions of both parties and the relevant constitutional provisions and case law.

Key point: Article 43 right to health is justiciable per this judgment.
